The Geospatial Computing & Society (GCS) Lab is dedicated to advancing geospatial technologies in ways that are socially responsible and beneficial. Our research spans spatial data science, geospatial artificial intelligence, and critical GIS, pursuing two interconnected directions: (1) developing computational and AI methods that address location privacy and social and geographic inequalities across digital spaces and policy contexts; and (2) engaging critical geography and GIS scholarship to examine the social implications of emerging geospatial technologies and envision alternative ways of how they can be designed and and developed.

**We're looking for prospective graduate and undergraduate students** interested in the intersections of spatial data science, GeoAI, spatial and social inequalities, and critical GIS. Prospective graduate students are encouraged to email me with their CV and review the GGIS Graduate Programs [application page](https://ggis.illinois.edu/academics/graduate-programs/apply).
